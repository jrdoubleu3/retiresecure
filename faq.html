Section 1: The Merton Method

What is the Merton Method?
▼
A smart, research-based way to decide how much of your money should go into stocks vs. safer investments—based on risk, expected returns, and your goals. It's not about timing the market. It's about sizing your investments in a disciplined way.
Is this active trading or market timing?
▼
Not at all. It's like passive investing, but with built-in risk awareness. You're still invested in low-cost index funds, just in a way that reflects your personal risk level and market conditions.
Why should I trust this method?
▼
It's based on economic research developed by a Nobel Prize–winning economist. It's the same logic used by many institutions—but adapted for individuals.

Section 2: How This Differs from Traditional Pensions

How is Pension 2.0 different from a traditional pension?
▼
Traditional pensions were offered by employers. Pension 2.0 is independent—it's yours. You contribute, we manage it professionally, and when you're ready, you get monthly income just like a pension.
Can I use this alongside my 401(k) or IRA?
▼
Yes! Pension 2.0 is designed to work with your other retirement accounts. It fills the gap that most modern plans leave open.
Who manages the investments?
▼
We do. Your money is invested using a professional, research-backed framework—like how university endowments manage their portfolios.
